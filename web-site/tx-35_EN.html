Meeting fishermen in Anita Conti's wake

Is it possible to eat sustainable fish?

“Who are these men, who live offshore in such difficult living conditions? How do these men tell the world? They feed the landlubbers, yet we know nothing about them...”
Anita Conti


Four crew members set sail from Brittany to Norway, following in the wake of the woman sailors nicknamed “the Lady of the Sea,” a leading figure in oceanography, literature, and photography who was already warning us about the limits of overfishing. Following in her footsteps, from port to port, we will meet fishermen and seafarers to raise awareness among the general public and young people about the crucial issues of sustainable fishing and highlight the beauty of the fishing profession.
The effects of overfishing are invisible to our eyes. Sustainable fishing is considered by scientists and ocean conservation organizations as a promising alternative for preserving marine ecosystems. It exists and deserves our full attention!

Details

A crew sets sail, following in the footsteps of Anita Conti, to retrace the journey of this oceanographer renowned for her work and commitment.
The crew will retrace her journey and, from port to port, highlight the initiatives that can give everyone the opportunity, on a daily basis, to actively contribute to preserving the marine environment.

Why?

Because the sea and the uses we make of it have a direct impact on the future of our planet and on the possibility for us and future generations to live there peacefully.

With whom / for whom?

Sailors, fishermen, and artisans, directly involved in both our food self-sufficiency and the maintenance of marine balances. Consumers (current or future) who have the power to act. When informed, they can help change our consumption habits.

How?

On the French coasts initially, then in the northern seas of Europe or along the coasts of North Africa, we will meet artisanal fishermen, their distribution networks and all the actors who support them in promoting their production (fishing methods, marketing circuits, redevelopment of sailing fishing, etc.). The project will show consumers and younger people (pupils / students / learners, etc.) the link between the act of consumption and the development of more sustainable fishing. It will highlight the relevance and possibility of consuming products from responsible fishing. The project includes moments of navigation and times for visits and meetings. The journey begins with a trip aboard a century-old sailing herring boat between the Gulf of Morbihan and the city of Lorient, which saw Anita's first baths and which houses a complete and consultable archive. The project is being developed with Laurent Girault Conti, Anita Conti's adopted son.
