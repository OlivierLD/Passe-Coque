The boat

Built in nearly 1000 units in the 1970s, this family keelboat was designed by Swedish architect Olle Enderlein. With a draft of 1.55m and a beam of only 2.60m, this small family cruising sailboat is an ideal partner for cruising along the coast. It is equipped with 4 to 5 beds: 2 people in the front cabin, 1 person in the rear bunk and 1 to 2 people can sleep in the saloon where the sliding dining table gives way to a final berth (potentially a double one...). The boat is equipped with toilets and a two-burner gas stove. A fridge container (not powered) but cleverly placed at the bottom of the hold keeps food cool.

Son histoire

Named Eh'Tak in tribute to the name given to the astral cartography used by the Polynesian peoples, this charming sailboat with its inimitable Nordic style has already had many lives. Its last owner, Werner Hesslauer - a doctor of Swiss-German origin, carried out major salvage work to put it back on its feet (the boat having been abandoned). Werner had to reluctantly part with the boat for family reasons with the wish that the boat continues to sail usefully with associations.

Associative projects

The boat is currently undergoing a refit, to accommodate a new gas stove and a gas connection (replacing a kerosene stove, considered too dangerous), as well as other upgrades and maintenance jobs.
The boat will join the Passe‑Coque Club from 2024.

Connection Wind turbine - Regulator

 The wind turbine is connected to the HRS-1000 regulator, And on top of that, it works!

Under the regulator, wires red, black, brown.

  • regulator red ⇒ blue
  • regulator black ⇒ green & yellow
  • regulator brown ⇒ brown

Derivation box : wires blue, green & yellow, brown.
Wind turbine output, blue : ⊖, brown : ⊕.
As mentionned in the manual, the wind turbine's brown ⊕ has to go on the brown in the derivation box, and the wind turbine's blue ⊖ goes on the green & yellow in the derivation box.
The fuse of the wind turbine is on the derivation box. 10-15A, 5x20 mm.
The current regulator is compatible with a solar panel, we'll look into it next.

Deep cleaning...

Steps:
  • Underwater:
    • Sanding the hull (band sander, grit 36!)
    • First coat, SOROMAP PR20, 4 × 300 ml (base 65%, hardner 35%)
    • Second coat, SOROMAP SC20, 2 × 300 ml (base 2, hardner 1)
    • Final coat, SOROMAP AFPro, hard matrix, dark blue, 2 coats. ~ 1.5 liters per coat.
  • Above water line:
    • Sanding the hull, grit 80
    • Sanding the teak toe rail
    • Sanding the exterior woodwork (cockpit, handrails on the roof)
    • First coat, SOROMAP PR20, 2 × 300 ml (base 65%, hardner 35%)
    • Second coat, SOROMAP SC20, 1 × 450 ml (base 2, hardner 1)
    • Final coat, 2 coats of SOROMAP SL2 (base 2, hardner 1).
      • Bleu Lazuli, 4 × 750 ml.
        One coat: ~ 450 ml.
      • Jaune Topaze, 750 ml (for the yellow line)
    • Oil for the outside woodwork.
      • That one, from AD
      • D1, Owatrol
      • Oil for teak, Syntilor
Note: The paint quantity calculator, from Soromap, doesn't work at all...
