The boat

The Jeanneau shipyard launched the first Sun Rise 34 (the Sun Rise 35 is the same, with a scoop) in 1985. This sailboat remained in the French shipyard's catalog for four years, with 629 units produced. This success is that of a well-born cruising boat, capable of racing with honors. It is true that this Fauroux design was launched just before the era when profitability required some sacrifices. The Jeanneau shipyard's constructions during this period were built to last, and this is evident today.

Jeanneau Sun Rise 35, a healthy and versatile sailboat

The Sun Rise 35 is a true fast cruiser, with a powerful hull, sound, balanced, and smooth to the helm. Its balance is conditional, however, on a fairly rapid reduction of sail (around 18 knots of wind) as the boat can heel over, but once the sail is reduced, the sailboat is very balanced and runs strong.

Indeed, this cruising sailboat is capable of taking its crew very far in remarkable sailing comfort. The Sun Rise 35 is suitable for coastal cruising as well as for offshore voyages.

The shipyard states:

"This boat exudes a powerful impression: the overall purity of its lines, the fine and aggressive waterlines, and the "champagne glass" hull section. The wide gangways make movement and maneuvering easy."

When the wind picks up, the Sun Rise is very comfortable in the breeze. It cuts well through the waves.
