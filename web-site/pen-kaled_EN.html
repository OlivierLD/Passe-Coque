Pen Kaled IV

Pen Kaled is a Gin Fizz ketch (almost like Nomaddict, which is a sloop) with a long history and quite a few miles under its keel.
Véronique and Denis form a crew, and together they've racked up an incredible amount of sailing experience between Greece and Polynesia, whether aboard Aquilon, Pen Kaled, or Yara. But the story began a long time ago... in Cap d'Agde, Passe-Coque didn't exist, and its president was just the skipper of an 8m Kelt (Adonis) on which Denis learned the basics of sailing off the Costa Brava. Another member of Passe-Coque was already there too... but that's another story!
It's also a family story. Pen Kaled and its crew of "sardines," with Coline — daughter of Denis and Véronique — at the helm, reunited with Yara and her parents in the Antilles before continuing their journey to the Pacific. After a detour to New York and Maine, a tragic event cast a shadow over this otherwise beautiful voyage with Yara's disappearance on the return trip from the Pacific. But Pen Kaled is still here, and Denis and Véronique have set sail again for an Atlantic circuit after a minor refit of the Gin Fizz, following the principles and values of Passe-Coque: to revive, pass on, and share. With Pordin Nancq's genoa sail and the outboard motor from "Au bout du vent", the beautiful ketch is now underway!
With these Gin Fizz boats, Pen Kaled and Nomadict, and the Arpèges, L'Heure Bleue, and Penny Lane, Passe-Coque continues its mission: to tell the story of sustainable and meaningful sailing.
